About

I am a Master’s student in Statistics at the University of Missouri–Columbia. My research spans Bayesian methods for high-dimensional data, decision-making models, and scientific software for neutron reflectometry. I am preparing PhD applications for Fall 2026.

Research

  • Bayesian kernel-based dimensionality reduction — thesis project on text embeddings and classification (advisor: S. Chakraborty).
  • Scientific software for neutron reflectometry — Qt/Python GUIs, motion control, live Plotly visualization at MU Research Reactor.
  • Keno probability models — undergraduate project on state lottery modeling (with M. McIntosh).

Publications & Manuscripts

  • Bayesian kernel-based dimensionality reduction for text embeddings (in progress).
  • Probability models for Keno draws (manuscript).
  • Conference posters and short papers available on request.

Teaching

Software & Projects
